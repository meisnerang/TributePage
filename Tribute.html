Ruth Bader Ginsburg

Equality Pioneer

Timeline

  • 1933 - Born Joan Ruth Bader in New York City, NY
  • 1954 - Graduates from Cornell University with a BA, first in her class
  • 1954 - Marries Martin D. Ginsburg
  • 1959 - Begins law career as as law clerk to a judge in the Southern District of New York
  • 1972 - Becomes the first woman to be hired with tenure at Columbia University School of Law
  • 1973 - Joins the ACLU as general counsel and founds the Women's Right's project with the ACLU
  • 1975 - Weinberger vs Weisenfield
  • 1980 - Appointed to the US Court of Appeals for the Disctict of Columbia Circuit by President Jimmmy Carter
  • 1993 - Appointed to the US Supreme Court by President Bill Clinton, becoming the second woman on the court
  • 2013 - Becomes the first Supreme Court justice to officiate at a same-sex marriage ceremony

"The words of the 14th Amendment’s equal protection clause—’nor shall any state deny to any person the equal protection of the laws.’ Well that word, ‘any person,’ covers women as well as men. And the Supreme Court woke up to that reality in 1971."

-- NPR
